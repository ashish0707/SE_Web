documented in april 1984 the united auto workers sued johnson controls on behalf of three employees these employees were mary craig who had chosen to be sterilized to avoid losing her job elsie nason a 50-year-old divorcee who had suffered a loss of compensation when she was transferred from a high paying job that exposed her to lead and donald penney who had been denied a request for a leave of absence for the purpose of lowering his blood lead levels because he intended to become a father the case was argued before the supreme court on october 10 1990 and was decided on march 20 1991 the
