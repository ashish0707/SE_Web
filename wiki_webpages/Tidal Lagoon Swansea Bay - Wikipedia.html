Tidal Lagoon Swansea Bay is a tidal lagoon power plant proposed to be constructed in Swansea Bay off the south coast of Wales, United Kingdom. Once constructed it would be the world's first tidal lagoon power plant.[1] This pathfinder project is planned to be the first of six tidal lagoon power plants to be constructed in the United Kingdom, and one of four planned to be built in Wales.[1]

In addition to proposals for a national fleet of lagoons which could meet up to 8% of UK electricity demand, or power for around 30% of UK homes, Tidal Lagoon Power is also progressing a number of potential projects overseas.

Tidal Lagoon Swansea Bay would have a capacity of 320MW,[2] power around 155,000 Welsh homes[3] and directly sustain over 2,200 construction and manufacturing jobs.[4]

It is also to be constructed to withstand 500-year-storms and to function as a coastline protection against storms and floods.[5]

The project was named as part of the UK government's 2014 National Infrastructure Plan[6] and was granted planning permission by the Department for Energy and Climate Change in June 2015.[1]
