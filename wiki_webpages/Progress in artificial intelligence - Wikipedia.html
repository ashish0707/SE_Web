Artificial intelligence applications have been used in a wide range of fields including medical diagnosis, stock trading, robot control, law, scientific discovery and toys. However, many AI applications are not perceived as AI: "A lot of cutting edge AI has filtered into general applications, often without being called AI because once something becomes useful enough and common enough it's not labeled AI anymore."[1] "Many thousands of AI applications are deeply embedded in the infrastructure of every industry."[2] In the late 1990s and early 21st century, AI technology became widely used as elements of larger systems,[2][3] but the field is rarely credited for these successes.

To allow comparison with human performance, artificial intelligence can be evaluated on constrained and well-defined problems. Such tests have been termed subject matter expert Turing tests. Also, smaller problems provide more achievable goals and there are an ever-increasing number of positive results.

Performance evaluation[edit]

The broad classes of outcome for an AI test are:

  • optimal: it is not possible to perform better
  • strong super-human: performs better than all humans
  • super-human: performs better than most humans
  • par-human: performs similarly to most humans
  • sub-human: performs worse than most humans

Optimal[edit]

See also: Solved game
  • Tic-Tac-Toe
  • Connect Four
  • Checkers[4]
  • Rubik's Cube[5]
  • Heads-up limit hold'em poker: statistically optimal in the sense that "a human lifetime of play is not sufficient to establish with statistical significance that the strategy is not an exact solution".[6] Sub-human for full ring Texas hold 'em; approaching strong super-human in simpler versions of poker.[7]

Strong super-human[edit]

  • Bridge: nearing strong super-human[8]
  • Chess: strong super-human: top human can compete only with handicap in their favor[9]
  • Go: strong super-human: beat a top human 4–1 in a five-game match in 2016[10]
  • Jigsaw puzzles: strong super-human[11]
  • Reversi: strong super-human[12]
  • Scrabble: strong super-human[13]
  • Quiz show question answering: strong super-human[14][15]

Super-human[edit]

  • Backgammon: super-human[16]
  • Crosswords: super-human[17]
  • Driving a car: super-human. Google driverless cars are safer and smoother when steering themselves than when a human takes the wheel.[18]
  • Arimaa: super-human[19]

Par-human[edit]

  • Optical character recognition for ISO 1073-1:1976 and similar special characters.
  • Classification of images[20]

Sub-human[edit]

  • Optical character recognition for printed text (nearing par-human for Latin-script typewritten text)
  • Handwriting recognition
  • Object recognition
  • Translation[21]
  • Speech recognition
  • Word-sense disambiguation
  • Natural language processing
